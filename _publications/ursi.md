This paper presents a statistical characterization of the segment (chunk) size distribution in Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP (DASH). Empirical distributions are derived from traffic captures of DASH video streams with varying qualities and segment durations, showing that Weibull and truncated logistic distributions accurately model chunk size behavior.
